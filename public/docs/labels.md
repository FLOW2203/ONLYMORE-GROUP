# COLHYBRI CITY and GOAT AME CITY, dual labels

> Twin labels distinguishing territories that have deployed an ONLYMORE Group ESS industrial cell.

Version: v2
Date: 2026-04-19

## COLHYBRI CITY

Tagline: Label des centres-villes qui font leur part.
Positioning: francophone and European.
Deployment targets: France, Belgium, Luxembourg, Switzerland, Italy, Portugal, Spain, bilingual Canadian cities.

The label is awarded by an independent committee after 6 months of cell operation and audit against the 5-flow framework. It is visible through a physical plaque at city hall, a digital badge on the city's web properties, and a referential entry in the ONLYMORE Group registry.

## GOAT AME CITY

Tagline: The greatest soul of all cities.
Positioning: US and English-speaking.
Deployment targets: US Rust Belt first (Detroit, Pittsburgh, Cleveland, Buffalo, Milwaukee, St Louis, Baltimore, Philadelphia), then expansion to UK, Ireland, Australia, urban Canada.

GOAT stands for "greatest of all time." AME is the soul. The label translates as "the greatest soul of all cities." Same criteria as COLHYBRI CITY, different positioning to fit local cultural codes.

## Shared criteria

Both labels share the same 5-flow criteria:
1. Active club SAS franchise operational at least 6 months
2. SCIC supporters registered and distributing dividends
3. COLHYBRI merchant tissue at least 20 active nodes
4. ONLYMORE FINANCE treasury interface open and operational
5. DOJUKU practice base with at least 100 students

Both labels are awarded by the same independent committee. A city can hold both labels simultaneously (relevant for bilingual territories like Montreal, Brussels or Geneva).

## Revocation

The label is awarded for 3 years and renewable. Revocation triggers: dormant SAS franchise, dissolved SCIC, fewer than 5 COLHYBRI merchants, closed DOJUKU base. Revocation is public.

## 2026 rollout

Detroit opens GOAT AME CITY Chapter 01 through the "C'est ma ville je m'engage" 2026 contest. Seven Rust Belt cities prepare 2027.

COLHYBRI CITY opens in parallel in Occitanie (France) during 2026, starting with the Rodilhan / Nimes metropolitan area.
